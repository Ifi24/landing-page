The World Studios main objective is to teach and research about aircraft and their engineering.

Check some of our researched airplanes

The Wright Flyer, sometimes referred to as Kitty Hawk, made the first sustained flight by a manned heavier-than-air powered and controlled aircraft—an airplane—on December 17, 1903. Invented and flown by brothers Orville and Wilbur Wright, it marked the beginning of the pioneer era of aviation.

The Cessna 172, one of the most classic aircraft measured by its longevity and popularity, the Cessna 172 is the most successful aircraft in history. Cessna delivered the first production model in 1956, and as of 2015, the company and its partners had built more than 44,000 units. Till today, the aircraft remains in production.

The Concorde was the first supersonic airliner, introduced in 1976 and retired in 2003, its only competitor was the Tupolev Tu-144, the Russian counterpart. Built by Aérospatiale (now Airbus) and British Aircraft Corporation (now BAE Systems) only 20 aircraft were produced.

The Airbus A380 was an airliner developed and produced by Airbus. It is the world's largest passenger airliner and the only airliner with a full-lenght double-deck. They were introduced in 2007 and retired in 2021, with 251 aircraft sold.
